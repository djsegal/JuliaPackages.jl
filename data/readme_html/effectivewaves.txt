<p>At present, the package focuses on materails filled with randomly placed particles. You can calculate effective wavenumbers for 2D [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1712.05427" rel="nofollow">1</a>] and 3D [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.00934" rel="nofollow">4</a>] acoustics, wave transimission and wave reflection in 2D [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1712.05427" rel="nofollow">1</a>,<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.10816" rel="nofollow">2</a>,<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/1905.06996" rel="nofollow">3</a>] and 3D [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.00934" rel="nofollow">4</a>], and scattering from an inhomogenious sphere [<a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.00934" rel="nofollow">4</a>]. See <a href="docs/src/theory/WavesInMultiSpecies.pdf">these notes</a> for brief formulas on effective wavenumbers.</p>
<p>Together with <a href="https://github.com/JuliaWaveScattering/MultipleScattering.jl">MultipleScattering.jl</a>, this package has been setup to easily extend to other dimensions, materials, and types of waves, such as elastic and electromagnetic waves.</p>
