<p dir="auto"><code>PoreMatMod.jl</code> is a <a href="https://julialang.org/" rel="nofollow">Julia</a> package for (i) subgraph matching and (ii) modifying crystal structures such as metal-organic frameworks (MOFs).</p>
<p dir="auto">Functioning as a "find-and-replace" tool on atomistic crystal structure models of porous materials, <code>PoreMatMod.jl</code> is useful for:</p>
<p dir="auto"><g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="hammer" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f528.png">ðŸ”¨</g-emoji> subgraph matching to filter databases of crystal structures</p>
<p dir="auto"><g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="hammer" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f528.png">ðŸ”¨</g-emoji> constructing hypothetical crystal structure models of functionalized structures</p>
<p dir="auto"><g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="hammer" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f528.png">ðŸ”¨</g-emoji> introducing defects into crystal structures</p>
<p dir="auto"><g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="hammer" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f528.png">ðŸ”¨</g-emoji> repairing artifacts of X-ray structure determination, such as missing hydrogen atoms, disorder, and guest molecules</p>
<p dir="auto">N.b. while <code>PoreMatMod.jl</code> was developed for MOFs and other porous crystalline materials, its find-and-replace operations can be applied to discrete, molecular structures as well by assigning an arbitrary unit cell.</p>
