<h1><a id="user-content-polychaos----orthogonal-polynomials-quadrature-and-polynomial-chaos" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#polychaos----orthogonal-polynomials-quadrature-and-polynomial-chaos"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>PolyChaos -- Orthogonal Polynomials, Quadrature, and Polynomial Chaos</h1>
<p>A Julia package to construct orthogonal polynomials, their quadrature rules, and use it with polynomial chaos expansions.</p>
<p>The package requires <code>Julia 1.0</code> or newer.
In <code>Julia</code> switch to the package manager</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> ]
(v1<span class="pl-c1">.0</span>) pkg<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> add PolyChaos</pre></div>
<p>This will install PolyChaos and its dependencies.
Once that is done, load the package:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-k">using</span> PolyChaos</pre></div>
<p>That's it.</p>
<p>Let's take a look at a simple example.
We would like to solve the integral</p>
<p><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/8eb4d29e06e9bf5622653055134c50ace1d9bfcc/68747470733a2f2f6c617465782e636f6465636f67732e636f6d2f6769662e6c617465783f253543696e745f30253545312532303625323078253545352532302535436d617468726d25374264253744782e"><img src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/8eb4d29e06e9bf5622653055134c50ace1d9bfcc/68747470733a2f2f6c617465782e636f6465636f67732e636f6d2f6769662e6c617465783f253543696e745f30253545312532303625323078253545352532302535436d617468726d25374264253744782e" alt="equation" data-canonical-src="https://latex.codecogs.com/gif.latex?%5Cint_0%5E1%206%20x%5E5%20%5Cmathrm%7Bd%7Dx." style="max-width:100%;"></a></p>
<p>Exploiting the underlying uniform measure, the integration can be done exactly with a 3-point quadrature rule.</p>
<pre lang="@example"><code>opq = Uniform01OrthoPoly(3)
integrate(x -&gt; 6x^5, opq)
</code></pre>
