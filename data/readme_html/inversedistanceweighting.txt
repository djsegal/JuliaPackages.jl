<p>This package provides a high-performance implementation of the inverse distance weighting scheme introduced in
the very early days of geostatistics (see <a href="https://dl.acm.org/citation.cfm?id=810616" rel="nofollow">Shepard 1968</a>).
It is perhaps the simplest first attempt in the literature to perform estimation based on the notion of proximity to data locations.</p>
<p>This implementation makes use of k-d trees from the <a href="https://github.com/KristofferC/NearestNeighbors.jl">NearestNeighbors.jl</a>
package, which leads to a fast estimation method for large or high-resolution spatial domains. Although this method is
recommended for fast assessment of a new field, it has poor statistical properties (lacks covariance model) and should
mainly be used for qualitative purposes.</p>
