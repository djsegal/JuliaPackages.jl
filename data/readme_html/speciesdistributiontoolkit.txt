<p dir="auto"><code>SpeciesDistributionToolkit.jl</code> is a collection of Julia packages forming a
toolkit meant to deal with (surprise!) species distribution data. Specifically,
the goal of these packages put together is to provide a consistent way to handle
occurrence data, put them on a map, and make it interact with environmental
information.</p>
<blockquote>
<p dir="auto">To get a sense of the next steps and help with the development, see the
<a href="https://github.com/orgs/PoisotLab/projects/3">issues/bugs tracker</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p dir="auto">This package is <em>not</em> intended to perform any actual modeling, but can serve as
a robust basis for such models. We offer an interface from this package to <code>MLJ</code>
to facilitate prediction.</p>
<p dir="auto">Form a technical point of view, this <em>repository</em> is a <a href="https://monorepo.tools/" rel="nofollow">Monorepo</a>
consisting of several related packages to work with species distribution data.
These packages were formerly independent and tied together with moxie and
<code>Require</code>, which was less than ideal. All the packages forming the toolkit share
a version number (which was set based on the version number of the eldest
package, <code>SimpleSDMLayers</code>), and the toolkit itself has its own version number.</p>
<p dir="auto">Note that the packages <em>do</em> work independently as well, but they are now <em>designed</em>
to work together. In particular, when installing <code>SpeciesDistributionToolkit</code>,
you get access to all the functions and types exported by the component
packages. This is the <em>recommended</em> way to interact with the packages.</p>

<p dir="auto"><strong>Getting occurrence data</strong>: <code>GBIF.jl</code>, a wrapper around the GBIF API, to
retrieve taxa and occurrence datasets, and perform filtering on these occurrence
data based on flags</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Getting environmental data</strong>: <code>SimpleSDMDatasets.jl</code>, an efficient way to
download and store environmental raster data for consumption by other packages.</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Using environmental data</strong>: <code>SimpleSDMLayers.jl</code>, a series of types and common
operations on raster data</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Simulating occurrence data</strong>: <code>Fauxcurrences.jl</code>, a package to simulate
realistic species occurrence data from a know series of occurrences, with
additional statistical constraints</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Getting organisms silhouettes</strong>: <code>Phylopic.jl</code>, a wrapper around the Phylopic
API</p>
