<h2><a id="user-content-overview" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#overview"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Overview</h2>
<p>CoordinateConverterGK.jl is a Julia package for conversion between the Cartesian coordinates and the geographic coordinates for a point with the Gauss-Krüger Projection. The formulae are based on documents and publications of <a href="https://www.gsi.go.jp/ENGLISH/index.html" rel="nofollow">the Geospatial Information Authority of Japan</a> (see <strong>References</strong>).</p>

<h2><a id="user-content-usage" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#usage"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Usage</h2>
<h3><a id="user-content-general-use" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#general-use"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>General use</h3>
<p>Given a central meridian <code>λ₀</code> and latitude of origin <code>φ₀</code> (in degrees), Cartesian coordinates can be converted with the following code:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre><span class="pl-k">using</span> CoordinateConverterGK
λ, φ <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">xy2lonlat</span>(λ₀, φ₀, easting, northing)</pre></div>
<p>where <code>λ</code> and <code>φ</code> are the converted longitude and latitude, <code>easting</code> and <code>northing</code> are the eastward and northward distances (in meters) from the origin, respectively.
A function <code>yx2latlon</code> is available in the same manner:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>φ, λ <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">yx2latlon</span>(φ₀, λ₀, northing, easting)</pre></div>
<p>Similarly, functions <code>lonlat2xy</code> and <code>latlon2yx</code> can convert geographic coordinates to Cartesian coordinates for a point.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>x, y <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">lonlat2xy</span>(λ₀, φ₀, λ, φ)</pre></div>
<h3><a id="user-content-japan-plane-rectangular-coordinate-system" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#japan-plane-rectangular-coordinate-system"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Japan Plane Rectangular Coordinate System</h3>
<p>When the Japan Plane Rectangular Coordinate System is adopted,
functions such as <code>xy2lonlat_ja</code> and <code>lonlat2xy_ja</code> can omit the coordinates of origin <code>λ₀</code>, <code>φ₀</code>.
These functions require the zone number of interest (1 to 19) instead.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>λ, φ <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">xy2lonlat_ja</span>(<span class="pl-c1">9</span>, easting, northing) <span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> in case of zone IX</span></pre></div>
