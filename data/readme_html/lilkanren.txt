<h2></h2>
<p>Just like Mitt, I'd like a binder full of kanren, and so I am collecting my assortment of notebooks on the topic in this repo.</p>
<p>Each notebook implements a (typically self-contained) kanren in a conversational style, while exploring a spectrum of design issues.  The code is meant mostly to be read and twiddled interactively.  At some later point the various lessons learned may be baked into library form suitable for public consumption.</p>

<h2></h2>
<p>The dogma is so designed to foster intuition about the geometry of search. The issue at hand is no different than the quandry of when to introduce calculators in grade school.</p>
<ul>
<li>Thou shalt not prophylactically <code>zzz</code>, lest you never overcome your fear of the Dragon.</li>
<li>Thou shalt not <code>reify</code>, lest your bones become brittle.</li>
<li>Thou shalt not <code>take</code> or <code>run</code> without first learning to <code>step</code>, so that you may better hear the gentle rhythms of <code>thunk</code>, <code>thunk</code>, <code>dribble</code>, <code>thunk</code>, <code>dribble</code>, <code>dribble</code>, <code>thunk</code>...</li>
<li>Thou shalt not <code>occursâœ“</code>, because we should not be afraid of our own shadow, but instead rejoice in our infinite potential.</li>
<li>Friends don't let friends use macros -- at least, not those made of sugar.  Macros made of acid may be a different story...</li>
</ul>
