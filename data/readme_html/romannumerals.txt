<p dir="auto">RomanNumerals.jl is a package that brings basic support for Roman numerals to Julia.</p>

<p dir="auto">RomanNumerals introduces one new type called <code>RomanNumeral</code>.</p>
<p dir="auto">You can create new Roman numerals by calling RomanNumeral("MMXV") or RomanNumeral(2015). Alternatively, you can simply write rn"MMXV" and Julia will interpret it correctly.</p>
<p dir="auto">Basic arithmetic operations are supported.</p>

<ul dir="auto">
<li>A forgiving parser:
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Leading and trailing whitespace is ignored</li>
<li>Can handle both lowercase and uppercase (and mixes of the two)</li>
<li>Can interpret non-optimal numerals (e.g. <code>XIIII</code>).
Non-optimal numerals were valid in traditional Rome, and even used more commonly than their minimal counterparts.
RomanNumerals.jl will automatically optimize non-optimal inputs.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Can do number theory (Who knew the Romans were into this kind of thing)</li>
<li>Fast</li>
</ul>
