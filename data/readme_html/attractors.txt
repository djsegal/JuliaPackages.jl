<p dir="auto">A Julia module for</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>finding attractors of arbitrary dynamical systems</li>
<li>finding their basins of attraction or the state space fractions of the basins</li>
<li>"continuing" the attractors and their basins over a parameter range</li>
<li>finding the basin boundaries and analyzing their fractal properties</li>
<li>tipping points related functionality for systems with known dynamic rule</li>
<li>and more!</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">It can be used as a standalone package, or as part of
<a href="https://juliadynamics.github.io/DynamicalSystems.jl/dev/" rel="nofollow">DynamicalSystems.jl</a>.</p>
<p dir="auto">To install it, run <code>import Pkg; Pkg.add("Attractors")</code>.</p>
<p dir="auto">All further information is provided in the documentation, which you can either find <a href="https://juliadynamics.github.io/Attractors.jl/dev/" rel="nofollow">online</a> or build locally by running the <code>docs/make.jl</code> file.</p>
<p dir="auto"><em>Previously, Attractors.jl was part of ChaosTools.jl</em></p>
