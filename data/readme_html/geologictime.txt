<p dir="auto">The code above generates the following image. In the argument list of <code>drawtimescale</code>, <code>100</code> and <code>0</code> are two ends of the time interval, representing time in million year ago; <code>[3, 4]</code> indicates the function to draw periods (code <code>3</code>) and epochs (code <code>4</code>) in the chart.</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://github.com/Mikumikunisiteageru/GeologicTime.jl/blob/master/docs/illust/imggts.png"><img src="https://github.com/Mikumikunisiteageru/GeologicTime.jl/raw/master/docs/illust/imggts.png" alt="Geologic time scale from 100 Ma ago to now" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">In addition, the package also provides simple lookup functions such as <code>getunit</code>, <code>getcolor</code>, <code>getstart</code>, <code>getstop</code>, <code>getspan</code>, and <code>getgeotime</code>, whose usage is explained in the <a href="https://Mikumikunisiteageru.github.io/GeologicTime.jl/dev" rel="nofollow">documentation</a>. These functions do not rely on PyPlot.jl --- they can be called once GeologicTime.jl is loaded.</p>
