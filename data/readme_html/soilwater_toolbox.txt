<p dir="auto"><strong>This package was developped by Joseph Pollacco and Jesús Fernández Gálvez</strong></p>
<p dir="auto">The SoilWater-ToolBox software is open source under the <strong>GP-3.0</strong> License and it includes a set of interlinked modules.  The SoilWater-ToolBox ecosystem is led by J.A.P Pollacco from Manaaki Whenua – Landcare Research in New-Zealand and J. Fernández-Gálvez from the University of Granada in Spain. The objectives of the SoilWater-ToolBox are to derive the soil hydraulic parameters by using a wide range of cost-effective methods. To date, the following modules are currently included into the SoilWater-ToolBox:</p>
<ol dir="auto">
<li>Intergranular Mixing Particle size distribution model: derives unimodal hydraulic parameters by using particle size distribution (Pollacco et al., 2020);</li>
<li>General Beerkan Estimation of Soil Transfer parameters method: derives the unimodal hydraulic parameters from single ring infiltration experiments (Fernández-Gálvez et al., 2019);</li>
<li>Sorptivity model: novel computation of sorptivity used in the General Beerkan Estimation of Soil Transfer parameters method (Lassabatere et al., 2021);</li>
<li>Derive saturated hydraulic conductivity from unimodal and bimodal <em>θ</em>(<em>ψ</em>) (Pollacco et al., 2017, 2013b);</li>
<li>Invert hydraulic parameters from <em>θ</em> time series (Pollacco et al. 2021);</li>
<li>Derive unique and physical bimodal Kosugi hydraulic parameters from inverse modelling using water retention and/or unsaturated hydraulic conductivity data directly measured in the laboratory or indirectly obtained from inverting <em>θ</em> time series (Fernández-Gálvez et al., 2021).</li>
</ol>
