<p dir="auto">JJDFTX.jl serves to bridge the gap between computational quantum chemistry (e.g. DFT) and photonics. The point of the package is to make easy the calculation of relevant physical quantities of materials that determine photonic properties such as plasmonic losses due to the electron-phonon interaction. Below, we show just a sample of the functionality of the package.</p>

<p dir="auto">We may also calculate the interband conductivity of a material like graphene through the same Fermi golden rule approach- interpolating matrix elements and energies across the Brillouin zone. The plot below may be cross checked with "Plasmonics in argentene" by Shankar et al.
The functionality is available through the "interbandsigma" method in the Boltzmann code.</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://github.com/AliGhorashiCMT/JJDFTX.jl/blob/main/imgs/interbandboltzmann.png"><img src="https://github.com/AliGhorashiCMT/JJDFTX.jl/raw/main/imgs/interbandboltzmann.png" alt="interband" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">In graphene, the velocity matrix elements are easy to compute, and should be equal to 3/2<em>a</em>t/hbar*[cos(theta), sin(theta)]. Where theta is the angle about the Dirac point. In JJDFTx, you may compute these elements by using the momentum_matrix_elements method. Note that this expects kpoints to be given in the lattice reciprocal basis. To make things convenient, one may use the normalize_kvector method to avoid converting from the cartesian basis explicitly.</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://github.com/AliGhorashiCMT/JJDFTX.jl/blob/main/imgs/GrapheneMomentum.png"><img src="https://github.com/AliGhorashiCMT/JJDFTX.jl/raw/main/imgs/GrapheneMomentum.png" alt="graphenemom" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
