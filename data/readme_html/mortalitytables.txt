<h3><a id="user-content-indexing" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#indexing"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Indexing</h3>
<p>Tables are indexed by a starting age and duration (even ultimate tables, under the hood). For tables with a starting age that is defined, but you've requested an attained age beyond the defined rates, you will get a <code>BoundsError</code>. If you ask for a starting age that is not defined, you will get a <code>missing</code>.</p>
<p>The rationale for this is, for example, this <a href="https://mort.soa.org/ViewTable.aspx?&amp;TableIdentity=1076" rel="nofollow">2001 CSO table</a> is not defined for ages 15 and under and ends at 120.</p>
<ul>
<li>You will get a <code>missing</code> if you ask for starting age 10 or 15, because it's plausible that you could encounter a a starting age not defined by a table.</li>
<li>You will get a <code>BoundsError</code> if you ask for an attained age 150 for someone select at age 16, because it is beyond the table's definition of its end.</li>
</ul>

<h4><a id="user-content-fractional-years" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#fractional-years"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Fractional Years</h4>
<p>When evaluating survival over partial years when you are given full year mortality
rates, you must make an assumption over how those deaths are distributed throughout
the year. Three assumptions are provided as options and are based on formulas
from the <a href="https://www.soa.org/globalassets/assets/Files/Research/2016-10-experience-study-calculations.pdf" rel="nofollow">2016 Experience Study Calculations paper from the SOA</a>, specifically pages 40-44.</p>
<p>The three assumptions are:</p>
<ul>
<li><code>Uniform()</code> which assumes an increasing force of mortality throughout the year.</li>
<li><code>Constant()</code> which assumes a level force of mortality throughout the year.</li>
<li><code>Balducci()</code> which assumes a decreasing force of mortality over the year. It seems <a href="https://www.soa.org/globalassets/assets/library/research/actuarial-research-clearing-house/1978-89/1988/arch-1/arch88v17.pdf" rel="nofollow">to
be for</a> making it easier to calculate successive months by hand.</li>
</ul>
<h5><a id="user-content-usage-1" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#usage-1"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Usage</h5>
<p>When you call a method below that uses the <code>time</code> argument (ie a period over which
you want to calculate the force of mortality), if you use a non <code>Int</code> (integer)
number then you need to specify the assumption as the last argument.</p>
<p>For example:</p>
<p><strong><em>Don't</em></strong> need to specify because you gave an <code>Int</code> time:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre><span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> calculate the 5-year survival for a person issued at age 50 and in</span>
<span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> the first duration</span>
issue_age <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">50</span>
duration <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">1</span>

<p>To add more tables for your use, download the <code>.xml</code> (aka the (<code>Xtbml</code> format)[<a href="https://mort.soa.org/About.aspx" rel="nofollow">https://mort.soa.org/About.aspx</a>]) version of the table from <a href="https://mort.soa.org" rel="nofollow">mort.SOA.org</a> and place it in the directory the package is installed in. This is usually <code>~user/.julia/packages/MortalityTables/[changing hash value]/src/tables/</code>. <em>Note: updating the package may remove your existing tables. Make a backup before updating your packages</em></p>
<p>After placing packages in the folder above, restart Julia and the should be discoverable when you run <code>mt.Tables()</code></p>
<p>If you would like more tables added by default, please open a GitHub issue with the request.</p>
