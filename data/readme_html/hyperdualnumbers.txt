<p dir="auto">Hyper-dual numbers can be used to compute first and second derivatives numerically without the cancellation errors of finite-differencing schemes.</p>
<p dir="auto">The initial Julia implementation (up to v3.0.1) is directly based on the C++ implementation by Jeffrey Fike and Juan J Alonso, both of Stanford University, department of Aeronautics and Astronautics as described in the paper:</p>
<p dir="auto"><a href="https://adl.stanford.edu/hyperdual/Fike_AIAA-2011-886.pdf" rel="nofollow">The Development of Hyper-Dual Numbers for Exact Second Derivative Calculations</a></p>
<p dir="auto">Up to v3.0.1 the Julia versions have been derived/written by Rob J Goedman (<a href="mailto:goedman@icloud.com">goedman@icloud.com</a>).</p>
<p dir="auto">HyperDualNumbers.jl v4.0.0 has been completely redone by Benoit Pasquier and follows the structure of the <a href="https://github.com/JuliaDiff/DualNumbers.jl">JuliaDiff/DualNumbers</a> package.</p>
<p dir="auto">For a quick intro, see <a href="https://github.com/JuliaDiff/HyperDualNumbers.jl/blob/master/STEPBYSTEP.md">STEPBYSTEP.md</a></p>
<p dir="auto">Latest tagged versions:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>v1.1.0 (Julia 0.5 &amp; 0.6, Oct 2017)</li>
<li>v2.0.0 (Julia v0.7-, Oct 2017)</li>
<li>v3.0.1 (Julia v0.7 &amp; Julia v1.0), Aug 2018, Pkg(3))</li>
<li>v4.0.0 (Julia v1.0, Nov 2018)</li>
<li>v4.0.9 (Julia 1.9-DEV, May 2022)</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">For details see <a href="https://github.com/JuliaDiff/HyperDualNumbers.jl/blob/master/VERSIONS.md">VERSION.md</a></p>
<p dir="auto">The following functions are specific to hyperdual numbers:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li><code>Hyper</code>,</li>
<li><code>Hyper256</code>,</li>
<li><code>Hyper128</code>,</li>
<li><code>ishyper</code>,y2</li>
<li><code>hyper_show</code></li>
<li><code>realpart</code>,</li>
<li><code>ε₁part()</code>, replaces eps1,</li>
<li><code>ε₂part()</code>, replaces eps2,</li>
<li><code>ε₁ε₂part()</code>, replaces eps1eps2</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">In the future it is my intention to deprecate:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li><code>hyper</code>,</li>
<li><code>hyper256</code>,</li>
<li><code>hyper128</code>,</li>
<li><code>eps1</code>,</li>
<li><code>eps2</code>,</li>
<li><code>eps1eps2</code></li>
</ul>
