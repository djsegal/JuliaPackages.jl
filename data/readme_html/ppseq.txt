<p dir="auto">This model aims to identify sequential firing patterns in neural spike trains in an unsupervised manner.
For example, consider the spike train below<sup>(1)</sup>:</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" href="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94763493-36bc0400-035f-11eb-95b7-40f583ed599b.png"><img src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94763493-36bc0400-035f-11eb-95b7-40f583ed599b.png" alt="image" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">By eye, we see no obvious structure in these data. However, by re-ordering the neurons according to PP-Seq's inferred sequences, we obtain:</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" href="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94767663-ee521580-0361-11eb-8729-de9eee1ab7d9.png"><img src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94767663-ee521580-0361-11eb-8729-de9eee1ab7d9.png" alt="image" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">Further, the model provides (probabilistic) assignment labels to each spike. In this case, we fit a model with two types of sequences. Below we use the model to color each spike as red (sequence 1), blue (sequence 2), or black (non-sequence background spike):</p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" href="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94767637-db3f4580-0361-11eb-89dd-28fd8a25c468.png"><img src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/636625/94767637-db3f4580-0361-11eb-89dd-28fd8a25c468.png" alt="image" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">The model is fully probabilistic and Bayesian, so there are many other nice summary statistics and plots that we can make. See our paper for full details.</p>
<p dir="auto"><strong>Footnote.</strong> (1) These data are deconvolved spikes from a calcium imaging recording from zebra finch HVC. These data were published in <a href="https://elifesciences.org/articles/38471" rel="nofollow">Mackevicius*, Bahle*, et al. (2019)</a> and are freely available online at <a href="https://github.com/FeeLab/seqNMF">https://github.com/FeeLab/seqNMF</a>.</p>
