<p dir="auto"><a href="https://travis-ci.org/jaak-s/BayesianDataFusion.jl" rel="nofollow"><img src="https://camo.githubusercontent.com/a9b2e65191aa5b3b2495692c13e48c93dfc6a8e8c607495583162712c1d9786b/68747470733a2f2f7472617669732d63692e6f72672f6a61616b2d732f426179657369616e44617461467573696f6e2e6a6c2e7376673f6272616e63683d6d6173746572" alt="Build Status" data-canonical-src="https://travis-ci.org/jaak-s/BayesianDataFusion.jl.svg?branch=master" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">Implementation of data fusion methods in Julia, specifically <strong>Macau</strong>, <strong>BPMF</strong> (Bayesian Probabilistic Matrix Factorization). Supported features:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Factorization of matrices (without or with side information)</li>
<li>Factorization of tensors (without or with side information)</li>
<li>Co-factorization of multiple matrices and tensors (any side information is
possible)</li>
<li>Side information inside relation</li>
<li>Parallelization (multi-core and multi-node)</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">BayesianDataFusion uses Gibbs sampling to learn the latent vectors and link
matrices. In addition to predictions Gibbs sampling also provides estimates
of <strong>standard deviation</strong> and possible other metrics (that can be computed from
samples).</p>
