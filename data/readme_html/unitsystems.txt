<p>Specifications for dimensional units are in the <a href="https://github.com/chakravala/UnitSystems.jl">UnitSystems.jl</a> and <a href="https://github.com/chakravala/UnitfulSystems.jl">UnitfulSystems.jl</a> repositories.
The two packages are designed so that they can be interchanged if compatibility with <a href="https://github.com/PainterQubits/Unitful.jl">Unitful.jl</a> is desired or not.
However, the <code>UnitfulSystems</code> package has fewer <code>UnitSystem</code> specifications available than the <code>UnitSystems</code> package due to limitations in combination with the <code>Unitful</code> package.
Specifically, <code>Metric</code>, <code>SI2019</code>, <code>CODATA</code>, <code>Conventional</code>, <code>MTS</code>, <code>EMU2019</code>, <code>English</code>, and <code>EnglishUS</code> can have <code>Unitful</code> values; while <code>Gauss</code>, <code>LorentzHeaviside</code>, <code>Thomson</code>, <code>EMU</code>, <code>ESU</code>, <code>ESU2019</code>, <code>IAU</code>, <code>FFF</code>, <code>Planck</code>, <code>PlanckGauss</code>, <code>Stoney</code>, <code>Hartree</code>, <code>Rydberg</code>, <code>Schrodinger</code>, <code>Electronic</code>, <code>Natural</code>, <code>NaturalGauss</code>, <code>QCD</code>, <code>QCDGauss</code>, and <code>QCDoriginal</code> are plain valued.</p>

<p>A <code>UnitSystem</code> is a consistent set of dimensional values selected to accomodate a particular use case or standardization.
It is possible to convert derived physical quantities from any <code>UnitSystem</code> specification into any other using accurate values.
In total, five fundamental constants <code>kB,ƒß,ùò§,Œº‚ÇÄ,m‚Çë</code> are used to specify a specific unit system.
These are the constants of <code>boltzmann</code>, <code>planckreduced</code>, <code>lightspeed</code>, <code>permeability</code>, and <code>electronmass</code>.
Different choices of natural units or physical measurements result in a variety of unit systems for many purposes.</p>
<p>Main documentation is at <a href="https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/units" rel="nofollow">https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/units</a></p>
<p>Another important additional definition is the <code>molarmass</code> constant <code>M·µ§</code>, which is automatically selected based on the choice of <code>boltzmann</code> constant (but can also be customized if necessary).
Historically, older electromagnetic unit systems also relied on a <code>rationalization</code> constant <code>Œª</code> and a <code>lorentz</code> force proportionality constant <code>Œ±L</code>.
In most unit systems these extra constants have a value of <code>1</code> unless otherwise specified.</p>

<p>Fundamental constants of physics are: <code>kB</code> Boltzmann's constant, <code>ƒß</code> reduced Planck's constant, <code>ùò§</code> speed of light, <code>Œº‚ÇÄ</code> vacuum permeability, <code>m‚Çë</code> electron rest mass, <code>Œª</code> Gauss rationalization, and <code>Œ±L</code> Lorentz's constant.
Primarily the <code>Metric</code> SI unit system is used in addition to the historic <code>English</code> engineering unit system.
These constants induce derived values for <code>avogadro</code>, <code>boltzmann</code>, <code>universal</code>, <code>planck</code>, <code>planckreduced</code>, <code>lightspeed</code>, <code>planckmass</code>, <code>atomicmass</code>, <code>protonmass</code>, <code>electronmass</code>, <code>newton</code>, <code>einstein</code>, <code>permeability</code>, <code>permittivity</code>, <code>coulomb</code>, and
additional constants <code>molarmass</code>, <code>hyperfine</code>, <code>luminousefficacy</code>, <code>stefan</code>, <code>radiationintensity</code>, <code>ampere</code>, <code>lorentz</code>, <code>biotsavart</code>, <code>rationalization</code>, <code>impedance</code>, <code>charge</code>, <code>magneton</code>, <code>conductance</code>, <code>faraday</code>, <code>magneticflux</code>, <code>josephson</code>, <code>klitzing</code>, <code>hartree</code>, <code>rydberg</code>, <code>bohr</code>, and <code>bohrreduced</code>.</p>
<p>Physics constant documentation is at <a href="https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/constants" rel="nofollow">https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/constants</a></p>
<p>Additional reference <code>UnitSystem</code> variants: <code>EMU</code>, <code>ESU</code>, <code>Gauss</code>, <code>LorentzHeaviside</code>, <code>MTS</code>, <code>SI2019</code>, <code>CODATA</code>, <code>Conventional</code>, <code>IAU</code>, <code>EnglishUS</code>; and natural atomic units based on gravitational coupling <code>Œ±G</code> and the fine structure <code>1/Œ±inv</code> constant (<code>Planck</code>, <code>PlanckGauss</code>, <code>Stoney</code>, <code>Hartree</code>, <code>Rydberg</code>, <code>Schrodinger</code>, <code>Electronic</code>, <code>Natural</code>, <code>NaturalGauss</code>, <code>QCD</code>, <code>QCDGauss</code>, and <code>QCDoriginal</code>).</p>
<p>Unt conversion documentation is at <a href="https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/convert" rel="nofollow">https://geophysics.crucialflow.com/dev/convert</a></p>
