<p dir="auto">KomaMRI.jl (formerly MRIsim.jl), whose name comes from the Japanese word for spinning-top こま (ko-ma) as they precess due to gravity like spins in a magnetic field.</p>
<p dir="auto">This package is meant to simulate general Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) scenarios that could arise in pulse sequence development.</p>
<blockquote>
<p dir="auto"><g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="green_circle" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f7e2.png">🟢</g-emoji> <strong>[6 Mar 2023] Paper published in MRM <g-emoji class="g-emoji" alias="smiley" fallback-src="https://github.githubassets.com/images/icons/emoji/unicode/1f603.png">😃</g-emoji>!</strong> The open access article is available <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/mrm.29635" rel="nofollow">here</a>.</p>

<p dir="auto">KomaMRI.jl comes with a handy GUI that contains a brain phantom with an EPI sequence. To open it use:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia notranslate position-relative overflow-auto" dir="auto" data-snippet-clipboard-copy-content="using KomaMRI
KomaUI()"><pre><span class="pl-k">using</span> KomaMRI
<span class="pl-c1">KomaUI</span>()</pre></div>
<p dir="auto">Press the button that says "Simulate!" to do your first simulation :). Then, a notification emerge telling you that the simulation was successful. In this notification, you can either select to (1) see the Raw signal or (2) to procced with the reconstruction.</p>
