<h2><a id="user-content-citation-and-reproducibility" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#citation-and-reproducibility"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Citation and Reproducibility:</h2>
<p>A preprint of our paper is available on <a href="https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/697755v2" rel="nofollow">bioRxiv</a>. If you use <code>MendelIHT.jl</code>, please cite:</p>
<pre><code>Benjamin B. Chu, Kevin L. Keys, Christopher A. German, Hua Zhou, Jin J. Zhou, Janet S. Sinsheimer, Kenneth Lange. Iterative Hard Thresholding in GWAS: Generalized Linear Models, Prior Weights, and Double Sparsity. bioRxiv doi:10.1101/697755
</code></pre>
<p>In the <code>figures</code> subfolder, one can find all the code to reproduce the figures and tables in our preprint. The <code>Project.toml</code> and <code>Manifest.toml</code> files can be used together to instantiate the exact same computing environment as was used in our paper. For more information about <code>.toml</code> files, please visit Julia's <a href="https://docs.julialang.org/en/v1.0/stdlib/Pkg/#Glossary-1" rel="nofollow">Pkg documentation</a>.</p>
