<p dir="auto">This example shows you how to build a simple Michaelis-Menten two-step enzyme catalysis model.</p>
<blockquote>
<p dir="auto">E + S ⇄ ES → E + P</p>
</blockquote>
<p dir="auto"><a href="https://pasmopy.readthedocs.io/en/latest/model_development.html" rel="nofollow"><code>pasmopy.Text2Model</code></a> allows you to build a BioMASS model from text. You simply describe biochemical reactions and the molecular mechanisms extracted from text are converted into an executable model.</p>
<p dir="auto">Prepare a text file describing the biochemical reactions (e.g., <code>michaelis_menten.txt</code>)</p>
<div class="snippet-clipboard-content notranslate position-relative overflow-auto" data-snippet-clipboard-copy-content="E + S ⇄ ES | kf=0.003, kr=0.001 | E=100, S=50

@obs Substrate: u[S]
@obs E_free: u[E]
@obs E_total: u[E] + u[ES]
@obs Product: u[P]
@obs Complex: u[ES]

@sim tspan: [0, 100]"><pre class="notranslate"><code>E + S ⇄ ES | kf=0.003, kr=0.001 | E=100, S=50
ES → E + P | kf=0.002
