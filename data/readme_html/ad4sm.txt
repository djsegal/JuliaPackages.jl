<p dir="auto"><em>Automatic Differentiation for Solid Mechanics in Julia</em></p>
<p dir="auto"><a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="/images/SpringFineMeshNHb.png"><img src="/images/SpringFineMeshNHb.png" height="280" style="max-width: 100%;"></a> <a target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" href="/images/3DSpringFineMeshNHb.png"><img src="/images/3DSpringFineMeshNHb.png" height="300" style="max-width: 100%;"></a></p>
<p dir="auto">This repository contains the following modules implementing an automatic differentiation system for the solution of solid mechanics problems in <a href="https://github.com/JuliaLang/julia">Julia</a>:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>adiff		    is the module implementing the automatic differentitation system in Julia</li>
<li>Materials     is the module implementing the strain energy evaluation functions for the materials</li>
<li>Elements      is the module for constructing the elements and for evaluating the deformation gradient at the integration points</li>
<li>Solvers       is the module implementing the energy evaluation functions for the single elements and for the model, and the functions for solving a finite element problem</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">The implementation of the forward mode automatic differentiation of this package is based on the <a href="https://github.com/JuliaDiff/ForwardDiff.jl">ForwardDiff.jl</a> package. The essential difference in the implementation developed here is that the calculation of the entries of the Hessian is explicitly addressed taking advantage of its simmetry, with the consequent improvement in computational cost.</p>

<p dir="auto">Automatic differentiation (AD) is an ensemble of techniques that allow to evaluate accurate numerical derivatives of a mathematical function expressed in a computer programming language.
In this study we use AD for stating and solving solid mechanics problems.
Given a finite element discretization of the domain, we evaluate the free energy of the solid  as the integral of its strain energy density, and we make use of AD for directly obtaining the residual force vector and the tangent stiffness matrix of the problem, as the gradient and the Hessian of the free energy respectively.
The result is a remarkable simplification in the statement and the solution of complex problems involving non trivial constraints systems and both geometrical and material non linearities.
Together with the continuum mechanics theoretical basis, and with a description of the specific AD technique adopted, the paper illustrates the solution of a number of solid mechanics problems, with the aim of presenting a convenient numerical implementation approach, made easily available by recent programming languages, to the solid mechanics community.</p>
