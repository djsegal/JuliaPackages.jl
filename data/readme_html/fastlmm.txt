<p dir="auto">Genetic analysis in structured populations used mixed linear models
where the variance matrix of the error term is a linear combination of
an identity matrix and a positive definite matrix.</p>
<p dir="auto">The linear model is of the familiar form: 𝑦 = 𝑋 β + ϵ.</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>𝑦: phenotype</li>
<li>𝑋: covariates</li>
<li>β: fixed effects</li>
<li>ϵ: error term</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">Further, V(ϵ) = τ²𝐾+ σ²𝐼, where τ² is
the genetic variance, σ² is the environmental variance, 𝐾
is the kinship matrix, and 𝐼 is the identity matrix.</p>
<p dir="auto">The key idea in speeding up computations here is that by rotating the
phenotypes by the eigenvectors of 𝐾 we can transform estimation to a
weighted least squares problem.</p>
<p dir="auto">This code is under development.</p>
