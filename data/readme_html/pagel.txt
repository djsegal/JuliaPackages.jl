<p dir="auto">A Julia implementation of the method described in:</p>
<p dir="auto">Pagel, Mark. "Detecting correlated evolution on phylogenies: a general method for the comparative analysis of discrete characters." <em>Proceedings of the Royal Society of London. Series B: Biological Sciences</em> 255.1342 (1994): 37-45.</p>
<p dir="auto">With some extensions that, in principle, allow you to model arbitrary numbers
of characters and states.</p>

<p dir="auto">The module exports a single function, <code>maxlikelihood</code>, whoose interface is:</p>
<div class="snippet-clipboard-content notranslate position-relative overflow-auto" data-snippet-clipboard-copy-content="maxlikelihood(model,treefile,statesfile)"><pre class="notranslate"><code>maxlikelihood(model,treefile,statesfile)
</code></pre></div>
<p dir="auto">Where model is one of <code>:dependant</code> or <code>:independant</code>, and <code>treefile</code> and <code>statefile</code>
are your data. <code>treefile</code> should just have a single newick string in it (not a NEXUS
file), states should be pretty much exactly what you would use for BayesTraits.</p>
