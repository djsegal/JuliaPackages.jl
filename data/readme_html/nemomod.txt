<p>NEMO is a high performance, open-source energy system optimization modeling tool developed in <a href="https://julialang.org/" rel="nofollow">Julia</a>.  It is intended for users who seek substantial optimization capabilities without the financial burden of proprietary software or the performance bottlenecks of common open-source alternatives. Key features of NEMO include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Least-cost optimization of energy supply and demand</li>
<li>Support for multiple regions and regional trade</li>
<li>Modeling of energy storage</li>
<li>Nodal network simulations and modeling of power and pipeline flow</li>
<li>Modeling of emissions and emission constraints (including carbon pricing and pollutant externalities)</li>
<li>Modeling of renewable energy targets</li>
<li>Support for simulating selected years in a modeling period</li>
<li>Parallel processing</li>
<li>Support for multiple solvers: <a href="https://projects.coin-or.org/Cbc" rel="nofollow">Cbc</a>, <a href="https://www.ibm.com/analytics/cplex-optimizer" rel="nofollow">CPLEX</a>, <a href="https://www.gnu.org/software/glpk/" rel="nofollow">GLPK</a>, <a href="https://www.gurobi.com/" rel="nofollow">Gurobi</a>, <a href="https://www.mosek.com/" rel="nofollow">Mosek</a>, and <a href="https://www.fico.com/en/products/fico-xpress-optimization" rel="nofollow">Xpress</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.sqlite.org/" rel="nofollow">SQLite</a> data store</li>
<li>Numerous performance tuning options</li>
</ul>
<p>NEMO can be used in command line mode or with the <a href="https://leap.sei.org/" rel="nofollow">Low Emissions Analysis Platform</a> (LEAP - formerly the Long-range Energy Alternatives Planning system) as a graphical user interface.</p>
<p>Development of NEMO is led by the Energy Modeling Program at the <a href="https://www.sei.org/" rel="nofollow">Stockholm Environment Institute (SEI)</a>.</p>
