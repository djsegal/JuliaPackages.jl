<p dir="auto">This package provides implementations of certain of the randomized numerical methods for linear algebra:</p>
<p align="center" dir="auto">
  minimize ‖<b><i>b</i></b> - <b><i>Ax</i></b>‖
</p>
It includes classical Kaczmarcz and coordinate descent methods.
<p dir="auto">The package also provides random projectors used to solve</p>
<p align="center" dir="auto">
  minimize ‖<b><i>Tb</i></b> - <b><i>TAx</i></b>‖
</p>
as an approximation of the initial system in the sense of the [Johnson–Lindenstrauss lemma](<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnson–Lindenstrauss_lemma" rel="nofollow">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johnson–Lindenstrauss_lemma</a>).

<p dir="auto">Some of state-of-art algorithms implemented are presented in the following paper.</p>
<blockquote>
<p dir="auto">Gower, Robert M., and Peter Richtárik. "Randomized iterative methods for linear systems." SIAM Journal on Matrix Analysis and Applications 36.4 (2015): 1660-1690.</p>
</blockquote>
