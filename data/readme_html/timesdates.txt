<p dir="auto">This package provides <code>TimeDate</code> to hold the date and time of day given in nanoseconds (or more coarsely).  And provides<code>TimeDateZone</code> to holds the the date and time of day in nanoseconds with a timezone. This work relies heavily on <code>Dates</code> and <code>TimeZones</code>; most of the attention to detail plays through.</p>
<p dir="auto"><code>Dates</code> has a <code>Time</code> type that has nanosecond resolution; it is not well supported, even within <code>Dates</code>.  This <code>Time</code> type recognizes strings only if they are limited to millisecond resolution. Only millisecond resolved times are relevant to <code>DateTime</code>s.  While at present limited by this millisecond barrier, <code>TimeZones</code> is a laudable package with active support.  I expect an eventual melding of what's best.</p>
<p dir="auto">Here, the inner dynamics rely upon the <code>Period</code> types (<code>Year</code> .. <code>Day</code>, <code>Hour</code>, .., <code>Nanosecond</code>) and <code>CompoundPeriod</code> all provided by <code>Dates</code>.  We distinguish <em>slowtime</em>, which is millisecond resolved, from a nanosecond resolved <em>fasttime</em>.</p>
<p dir="auto">The general approach is separate the date, slowtime, fasttime, and timezone (if appropriate), then use the date, slowtime and timezone (if appropriate) to obtain a coarse result using the facilities provided by the <code>Date</code> and <code>TimeZones</code> packages.  We refine the coarse result by adding or subtracting the fasttime, as appropriate.</p>
