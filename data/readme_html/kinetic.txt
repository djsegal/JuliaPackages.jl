<p dir="auto"><strong>Kinetic</strong> is a computational fluid dynamics toolbox written in <a href="https://julialang.org" rel="nofollow"><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuliaLang/julia-logo-graphics/master/images/julia.ico" width="16em" style="max-width: 100%;">Julia.</a>
It aims to furnish efficient modeling and simulation methodologies for fluid dynamics, augmented by the power of machine learning.
Based on differentiable programming, mechanical and neural network models are fused and solved in a unified framework.
Simultaneous 1-3 dimensional numerical simulations can be performed on CPUs and GPUs.</p>
<p dir="auto">The ecosystem follows the modular design philosophy.
Depending on the specific use case, the main module is split into portable components to reduce the lantency caused by the LLVM <a href="https://llvm.org/docs/tutorial/index.html#building-a-jit-in-llvm" rel="nofollow">just-in-time</a> compiler:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/KitBase.jl">KitBase</a>: physical models and numerical schemes</li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/KitML.jl">KitML</a>: neural models and machine learning methods</li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/KitFort.jl">KitFort</a>: optional high-performance Fortran backend</li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/FluxReconstruction.jl">FluxReconstruction</a>: high-fidelity solution algorithms</li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/Langevin.jl">Langevin</a>: intrusive uncertainty quantification methods</li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/vavrines/kineticpy">kineticpy</a>: Python interface built on top of <a href="https://github.com/JuliaPy/pyjulia">pyjulia</a></li>
</ul>

<p dir="auto">Kinetic models and simulates fluid dynamics problems from the perspective of particle transport.
Any advection-diffusion-type equation of different particles, including molecules, photons, plasmas, neutrons, etc., can be solved within the framework.
Special attentions have been paid on Hilbert's sixth problem, i.e. to build the numerical passage between kinetic theory of gases, e.g. the Boltzmann equation, and continuum mechanics, e.g. the Euler and Navier-Stokes equations.
A partial list of current supported models and equations include:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Boltzmann equation</li>
<li>radiative transfer equation</li>
<li>Fokker-Planck-Landau equation</li>
<li>direct simulation Monte Carlo</li>
<li>advection-diffusion equation</li>
<li>Burgers equation</li>
<li>Euler equations</li>
<li>Navier-Stokes equations</li>
<li>Magnetohydrodynamical equations</li>
<li>Maxwell's equations</li>
</ul>
