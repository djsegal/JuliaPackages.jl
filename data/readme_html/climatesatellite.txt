<p dir="auto"><code>ClimateSatellite.jl</code> is a Julia package that aims to streamline the following processes:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>downloading of climate satellite mission data</li>
<li>basic analysis (mean, maximum, minimum, standard deviation, etc.) of downloaded data</li>
<li>extraction of data for a given <strong>GeoRegion</strong> (see <code>GeoRegions.jl</code> for more information)</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto"><code>ClimateSatellite.jl</code> can be installed via</p>

</code></pre></div>

<p dir="auto">Since my research work focuses mainly on precipitation and water vapour research, my efforts will mostly be on satellite missions involving these variables (i.e. PMM (GPM/TRMM) and MIMIC-TPW2m).  However, if you are interested in adding scripts for additional missions, feel free to submit a pull request.</p>
<p dir="auto"><code>ClimateSatellite.jl</code> currently supports the retrieval of data from the following satellites/
missions:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Global Precipitation Mission (GPM) Research Product (GPM-FINAL)</li>
<li>Global Precipitation Mission (GPM) Late NRT Product (GPM-LATE)</li>
<li>Global Precipitation Mission (GPM) Early NRT Product (GPM-EARLY)</li>
<li>Morphed Integrated Microwave Imagery at CIMSS (MIMIC)-TPW2m (MIMIC)</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">The retrieval of the following satellites/missions are in development:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Tropical Rainfall Measuring Mission (TRMM)</li>
<li>Remote Sensing Systems TM (RSS)</li>
<li>MODerate resolution Imaging Spectroradiometer (MODIS)</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">A list of satellite data sources and their products (because some have data for multiple product types), can be found in the <code>satellites.txt</code> file, along with their properties, units of measurement, etc.  However, only the following combinations of Missions / Products are currently valid in ClimateSatellite - all other options will throw an error.</p>
