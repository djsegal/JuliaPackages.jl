<p dir="auto">Prototype differentiable simulator for robotic systems. This repository includes models for planar hopper, biped, and quadruped systems.</p>
<p dir="auto">Systems are represented in minimal coordinates and contact impulses are computed at each time step by solving a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_complementarity_problem" rel="nofollow">nonlinear complementarity problem (NCP)</a> using a custom <a href="src/solver/interior_point.jl">interior-point solver</a>. Gradients through the dynamics are efficiently computed via the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicit_function_theorem" rel="nofollow">implicit-function theorem</a> applied to the NCP optimality conditions.</p>
<p dir="auto">For more details, see our paper: <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.00806" rel="nofollow">Dojo: A Differentiable Simulator for Robotics</a> and checkout our full simulator: <a href="https://github.com/dojo-sim/Dojo.jl">Dojo</a>.</p>
