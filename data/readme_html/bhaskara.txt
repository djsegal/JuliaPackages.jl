<p>Provides Bhaskara I's sine approximation formula</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre><span class="pl-en">bsin</span>(θ) <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">16</span>(π<span class="pl-k">-</span>θ)θ <span class="pl-k">/</span> (<span class="pl-c1">5</span>π<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span> <span class="pl-k">-</span> <span class="pl-c1">4</span>(π<span class="pl-k">-</span>θ)θ)      <span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> error &lt; 0.002, θ ∈ [0,π]</span></pre></div>
<p>There is also <code>b2sin(θ)</code> for <code>θ ∈ [-π,π]</code>, and <code>Bhaskara.sin(θ)</code> for <code>θ ∈ 𝐑</code>,
by inserting appropriate <code>mod(,2π)</code> and <code>sign()*abs()</code> bits.
Similarly <code>bcos(θ)</code> is for <code>θ ∈ [-π/2,π/2]</code>, and <code>Bhaskara.cos(θ)</code> for all <code>θ ∈ 𝐑</code>.</p>
