<p dir="auto">This is a package for Structural Equation Modeling.
It is still <em>in development</em>.
Models you can fit include</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Linear SEM that can be specified in RAM (or LISREL) notation</li>
<li>ML, GLS and FIML estimation</li>
<li>Ridge Regularization</li>
<li>Multigroup SEM</li>
<li>Sums of arbitrary loss functions (everything the optimizer can handle).</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">We provide fast objective functions, gradients, and for some cases hessians as well as approximations thereof.
As a user, you can easily define custom loss functions.
For those, you can decide to provide analytical gradients or use finite difference approximation / automatic differentiation.
You can choose to mix and match loss functions natively found in this package and those you provide.
In such cases, you optimize over a sum of different objectives (e.g. ML + Ridge).
This mix and match strategy also applies to gradients, where you may supply analytic gradients or opt for automatic differentiation or mix analytical and automatic differentiation.</p>
<p dir="auto">You may consider using this package if:</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>you want to extend SEM (e.g. add a new objective function) and need an extendable framework</li>
<li>you want to extend SEM, and your implementation needs to be fast (because you want to do a simulation, for example)</li>
<li>you want to fit the same model(s) to many datasets (bootstrapping, simulation studies)</li>
<li>you are planning a study and would like to do power simulations</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">The package makes use of</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>Symbolics.jl for symbolically precomputing parts of the objective and gradients to generate fast, specialized functions.</li>
<li>SparseArrays.jl to speed up symbolic computations.</li>
<li>Optim.jl and NLopt.jl to provide a range of different Optimizers/Linesearches.</li>
<li>FiniteDiff.jl and ForwardDiff.jl to provide gradients for user-defined loss functions.</li>
</ul>
<p dir="auto">At the moment, we are still working on</p>
<ul dir="auto">
<li>optimizing performance for big models (with hundreds of parameters)</li>
</ul>
