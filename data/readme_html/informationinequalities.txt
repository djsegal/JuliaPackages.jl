<p dir="auto">This package contains routines to perform Information Theoretic measures. The priliminary build up of this tool is to validate, prove and analyze Information Inequalities. This can be used both for rigorous computations and analysis of Information measures and expressions.</p>

<p dir="auto">If you spot something strange in the software (something doesn't work or doesn't behave as expected) do not hesitate to open a <a href="https://github.com/InformationInequalities.jl/issues/new?assignees=&amp;labels=bug&amp;template=bug_report.md&amp;title=%5BBUG%5D">bug issue</a>.</p>
<p dir="auto">If have an idea of how to make the package better (a new feature, a new piece of documentation, an idea to improve some existing feature), you can open an <a href="https://github.com/InformationInequalities.jl/issues/new?assignees=&amp;labels=enhancement&amp;template=feature_request.md&amp;title=%5Bfeature+request%5D%3A+">enhancement issue</a>.</p>
<p dir="auto">If you feel like your issue does not fit any of the above mentioned templates (e.g. you just want to ask something), you can also open a <a href="https://github.com/InformationInequalities.jl/issues/new">blank issue</a>.</p>
<p dir="auto">Pull requests are also very welcome! More details in the <a href="https://nivupai.github.io/InformationInequalities.jl/stable/CONTRIBUTING/" rel="nofollow">contributing guidelines</a></p>
