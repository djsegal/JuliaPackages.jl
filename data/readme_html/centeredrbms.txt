<p dir="auto">Train and sample centered Restricted Boltzmann machines in Julia. See [Melchior et al] for the definition of <em>centered</em>. Consider an RBM with binary units. Then the centered variant has energy defined by:</p>
<p dir="auto"><math-renderer class="js-display-math" style="display: block" data-static-url="https://github.githubassets.com/static" data-run-id="8c9a8ca305a659fadd19f4a14b4df37e">$$
E(v,h) = -\sum_i a_i v_i - \sum_\mu b_\mu h_\mu - \sum_{i\mu} w_{i\mu} (v_i - c_i) (h_\mu - d_\mu)
$$</math-renderer></p>
<p dir="auto">with offset parameters <math-renderer class="js-inline-math" style="display: inline" data-static-url="https://github.githubassets.com/static" data-run-id="8c9a8ca305a659fadd19f4a14b4df37e">$c_i,d_\mu$</math-renderer>. Typically <math-renderer class="js-inline-math" style="display: inline" data-static-url="https://github.githubassets.com/static" data-run-id="8c9a8ca305a659fadd19f4a14b4df37e">$c_i,d_\mu$</math-renderer> are set to approximate the average activities of <math-renderer class="js-inline-math" style="display: inline" data-static-url="https://github.githubassets.com/static" data-run-id="8c9a8ca305a659fadd19f4a14b4df37e">$v_i$</math-renderer> and <math-renderer class="js-inline-math" style="display: inline" data-static-url="https://github.githubassets.com/static" data-run-id="8c9a8ca305a659fadd19f4a14b4df37e">$h_\mu$</math-renderer>, respectively, as this seems to help training (see [Montavon et al]).</p>
