<p>Implement one-way and multi-way anova, including type 1, 2 and 3 sum of squares. The syntax and output resemble package <code>GLM</code>.
The types of models supported:</p>
<ol>
<li><code>TableRegressionModel{&lt;: LinearModel, T}</code> fit by <code>GLM.lm</code></li>
<li><code>LinearMixedModel</code> fit by <code>MixedAnova.lme</code> or <code>fit(LinearMixedModel, ...)</code></li>
<li><code>TableRegressionModel{&lt;: GeneralizedLinearModel, T}</code> fit by <code>GLM.glm</code></li>
</ol>

<p>Noticed that <code>anova</code> choose likelihood-ratio test automatically by detecting the type of model. For families of <code>Bernoulli()</code>, <code>Binomial()</code>, <code>Poisson()</code> that have fixed dispersion and <code>LinearMixedModel</code>, likelihood-ratio test is preferred. For other models, F test is preferred. To specify the test, we can use <code>anova(models..., test = &lt;test&gt;)</code> or <code>anova(&lt;test&gt;, models...)</code>. Use <code>FTest</code> for F test, <code>LikelihoodRatioTest</code> or <code>LRT</code> for likelihood-ratio test.</p>
