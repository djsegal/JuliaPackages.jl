<p dir="auto"><code>DateSelectors.jl</code> simplifies the partitioning of a collection of dates into non-contiguous validation and holdout sets in line with best practices for tuning hyper-parameters, for time-series machine learning.</p>
<p dir="auto">The package exports the <code>partition</code> function, which assigns dates to the validation and holdout sets according to the <code>DateSelector</code>.
The available <code>DateSelector</code>s are:</p>
<ol dir="auto">
<li><code>NoneSelector</code>: assigns all dates to the validation set.</li>
<li><code>RandomSelector</code>: randomly draws a subset of dates <em>without</em> replacement.</li>
<li><code>PeriodicSelector</code>: draws contiguous subsets of days periodically from the collection.</li>
</ol>
<p dir="auto">A notable trait of the <code>DateSelector</code>s is that the selection is invariant to the start and end-dates of collection itself.
Thus you can shift the start and end dates, e.g. by a week, and the days in the overlapping period will consitently still be placed into holdout or validation as before.
The only thing that controls if a date is selected or not is the parameters of the <code>DateSelector</code> itself.</p>
<p dir="auto">See the <a href="https://invenia.github.io/DateSelectors.jl/stable/examples.html" rel="nofollow">examples</a> in the docs for more info.</p>
