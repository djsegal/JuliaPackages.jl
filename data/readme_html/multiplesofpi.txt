<h1><a id="user-content-introduction" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#introduction"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Introduction</h1>
<p>This package exports the type <code>PiExpTimes{N,T}</code> that satisfies <code>PiExpTimes{N,T}(x) = x*π^N</code>, and the type <code>PiTimes</code> that is aliased to <code>PiExpTimes{1}</code>. It also provides the constant <code>Pi</code> for convenience, defined as <code>PiTimes(1)</code>, that behaves like <code>π</code> except it produces results with higher accuracy in certain trigonometric and algebraic contexts. In most scenarios the numbers <code>Pi</code> and <code>π</code> are interchangable.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span> <span class="pl-k">==</span> π<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>
<span class="pl-c1">true</span></pre></div>
<p>Expressing mathematical relations in terms of <code>Pi</code> instead of the more cumbersome <code>PiExpTimes</code> is usually cleaner, and is recommended unless it's specifically necessary to do otherwise. One such scenario is exponentiation, more on which is presented below.</p>
<h2><a id="user-content-rationale" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#rationale"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Rationale</h2>
<p>The number <code>π</code> is represented as an <code>Irrational</code> type in julia, and may be computed to an arbitrary degree of precision. In normal course of events it is converted to a float when it encounters another number, for example <code>2π</code> is computed by converting both <code>2</code> and <code>π</code> to floats and subsequently carrying out a floating-point multiplication. This is lossy, as both <code>2</code> and <code>π</code> may be represented with arbitrary precision. This package delays the conversion of the <code>π</code> to a float, treating it as a common factor in algebraic simplifications. This limits floating-point inaccuracies, especially if the terms multiplying <code>π</code> are exactly representable in binary. As an added advantage, it uses <code>sinpi</code> and <code>cospi</code> wherever possible to avoid having to convert <code>π</code> to a float altogether.</p>

<h3><a id="user-content-type-instability" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#type-instability"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Type-instability</h3>
<p>The type <code>PiExpTimes{N}</code> stores the exponent as a type-parameter, therefore exponentiation is not type-stable in general.</p>
<h3><a id="user-content-constructor-abuse-to-avoid-nesting" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#constructor-abuse-to-avoid-nesting"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Constructor-abuse to avoid nesting</h3>
<p><code>PiExpTimes{N}(PiExpTimes{M})</code> is automatically simplified to <code>PiExpTimes{N+M}</code>. This is an abuse of Julia's constructors as the type of the object changes, however this avoids nested expressions that have performance issues.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">PiExpTimes</span><span class="pl-c1">{2}</span>(<span class="pl-c1">PiExpTimes</span><span class="pl-c1">{3}</span>(<span class="pl-c1">4</span>))
<span class="pl-c1">4</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">5</span>

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">PiExpTimes</span><span class="pl-c1">{2}</span>(<span class="pl-c1">PiExpTimes</span><span class="pl-c1">{3}</span>(<span class="pl-c1">4</span>)) <span class="pl-k">|&gt;</span> typeof
PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">5</span>,Int64}</pre></div>
<h3><a id="user-content-interactions-with-π" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#interactions-with-π"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Interactions with π</h3>
<p>The irrational number <code>π</code> is usually aggressively converted to <code>PiTimes(1)</code>, eg:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">PiTimes</span>(π)
Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span></pre></div>
<p>This ensures that subsequent calculation would not get promoted to a floating-point type. However if this behavior is not desired then one may specify the type explicitly while constructing the object as</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">PiTimes</span><span class="pl-c1">{Irrational{:π}}</span>(π)
π <span class="pl-k">=</span> <span class="pl-c1">3.1415926535897</span><span class="pl-k">...</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi</pre></div>
<p>However, it is not possible to convert a number to the type <code>PiTimes{Irrational{:π}}</code>.</p>
<h3><a id="user-content-floating-point-promotion" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#floating-point-promotion"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Floating-point promotion</h3>
<p>Addition and subtraction involving mixed exponents of <code>Pi</code> will involve floating-point conversions, and the resulting expression will have the minimum exponent out of the terms being summed.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> Pi <span class="pl-k">+</span> <span class="pl-c1">3</span>Pi
<span class="pl-c1">4</span>Pi

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> Pi <span class="pl-k">+</span> <span class="pl-c1">3</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>
<span class="pl-c1">10.42477796076938</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi</pre></div>
<p>This fits with the intuition of the expression being factorized as <code>Pi + 3Pi^2 == Pi*(1 + 3Pi)</code>.</p>
<p>Note that <code>π</code> is promoted to <code>Pi</code> in such operations, so we obtain</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> Pi <span class="pl-k">+</span> π
<span class="pl-c1">2</span>Pi</pre></div>
<h3><a id="user-content-conversion-vs-construction" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#conversion-vs-construction"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Conversion vs construction</h3>
<p>Constructors for <code>PiExpTimes</code> act as a wrapper and not as a conversion. Conversion to the type retains the value of a number, whereas construction implies multiplication by an exponent of <code>Pi</code>.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">PiTimes</span>(<span class="pl-c1">1</span>)
Pi

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">convert</span>(PiTimes,<span class="pl-c1">1</span>)
Pi<span class="pl-k">^-</span><span class="pl-c1">1</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi</pre></div>
<h3><a id="user-content-promotion-of-mixed-types" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#promotion-of-mixed-types"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Promotion of mixed types</h3>
<p><code>promote</code> and <code>promote_type</code> work differently with types having different exponents. <code>promote</code> converts both the types to one that has the minimum exponent, whereas <code>promote_type</code> leaves the exponent as a free parameter.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">promote</span>(Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>) <span class="pl-k">|&gt;</span> typeof
Tuple{PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">1</span>,Real},PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">1</span>,Real}}

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">promote_type</span>(<span class="pl-c1">typeof</span>(Pi),<span class="pl-c1">typeof</span>(Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>))
PiExpTimes{N,Int64} <span class="pl-k">where</span> N</pre></div>
<p>This is so that structs of <code>PiTimes</code> — such as complex numbers — do not lose accuracy in conversion. The behaviour is explained below with some examples:</p>
<h4><a id="user-content-arrays-of-mixed-types" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#arrays-of-mixed-types"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Arrays of mixed types</h4>
<p>Storing different exponents of <code>Pi</code> in an array will in general lead to conversion to a supertype that can store all the values.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> [Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>] <span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> element type is not concrete</span>
<span class="pl-c1">2</span><span class="pl-k">-</span>element Array{PiExpTimes{N,Int64} <span class="pl-k">where</span> N,<span class="pl-c1">1</span>}<span class="pl-k">:</span>
   Pi
 Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span></pre></div>
<p>Such an array will not be the most performant, as the element type is not concrete. This may be avoided by specifying a type while creating the array. A concrete type will not be able to store all the numbers losslessly.</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">2</span>,Real}[Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>] <span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> exponent is fixed but Real is not a concrete type</span>
<span class="pl-c1">2</span><span class="pl-k">-</span>element Array{PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">2</span>,Real},<span class="pl-c1">1</span>}<span class="pl-k">:</span>
 Pi<span class="pl-k">^-</span><span class="pl-c1">1</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>
       Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">2</span>,Float64}[Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>] <span class="pl-c"><span class="pl-c">#</span> eltype is concrete, but result loses accuracy</span>
<span class="pl-c1">2</span><span class="pl-k">-</span>element Array{PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">2</span>,Float64},<span class="pl-c1">1</span>}<span class="pl-k">:</span>
 <span class="pl-c1">0.3183098861837907</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>
                    Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span></pre></div>
<h4><a id="user-content-complex-numbers" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#complex-numbers"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Complex numbers</h4>
<p>Complex numbers rely on <code>promote</code> to generate the element type</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">Complex</span>(Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>)
Pi <span class="pl-k">+</span> Pi<span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">*</span>im

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">Complex</span>(Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>) <span class="pl-k">|&gt;</span> typeof
Complex{PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">1</span>,Real}}</pre></div>
<p>In this case converting either the real or imaginary part to a floating-point type would have resulted in a loss of accuracy. Such a type might not be performant, so if a conversion is desired then it might be enforced by specifying the element type while constructing the <code>Complex</code> struct:</p>
<div class="highlight highlight-source-julia"><pre>julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">Complex</span><span class="pl-c1">{PiTimes{Float64}}</span>(Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>)
Pi <span class="pl-k">+</span> <span class="pl-c1">3.141592653589793</span><span class="pl-k">*</span>Pi<span class="pl-k">*</span>im

julia<span class="pl-k">&gt;</span> <span class="pl-c1">Complex</span><span class="pl-c1">{PiTimes{Float64}}</span>(Pi,Pi<span class="pl-k">^</span><span class="pl-c1">2</span>) <span class="pl-k">|&gt;</span> typeof
Complex{PiExpTimes{<span class="pl-c1">1</span>,Float64}}</pre></div>
